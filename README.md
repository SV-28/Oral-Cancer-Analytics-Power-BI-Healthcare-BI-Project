# Oral-Cancer-Analytics-Power-BI-Healthcare-BI-Project

**📌 Project Overview**

Oral cancer remains a major public health concern worldwide, with survival outcomes heavily influenced by early diagnosis, tumor size, risk factors, and treatment type.

This project analyzes multi-country oral cancer data to:

Identify diagnosis trends by age & gender

Examine high-risk factors across age groups

Analyze survival rate vs cancer stage

Compare treatment costs across countries

Understand tumor size distribution in diagnosed cases

The final output is an interactive Power BI dashboard designed for clinical researchers, healthcare administrators, and public health analysts.

**🎯 Business Problem**

Healthcare organizations need data-driven insights to:

Detect high-risk populations early

Improve survival outcomes

Optimize treatment planning

Understand cost variations across regions

Support policy and preventive screening programs

This dashboard transforms raw clinical data into actionable healthcare intelligence.

**📈 Key Insights**

1️⃣ Age & Diagnosis Trends

Highest diagnosis rates observed in 45–59 age group (53%)

Younger (<30) and elderly (>75) groups show lower but significant risk

Diagnosis rate consistent across male and female (~50%)

2️⃣ Survival vs Cancer Stage

Stage 0 shows highest 5-year survival rate

Survival rate drops sharply from Stage 1 onward

Treatment cost increases significantly with advanced stages

 Early detection dramatically improves outcomes and reduces financial burden.

3️⃣ High-Risk Factors by Age

Heatmap analysis shows strong association between:

Tobacco use

Alcohol consumption

Poor oral hygiene

HPV infection

Older age groups show higher combined risk factor prevalence.

4️⃣ Treatment Cost Analysis

Chemotherapy, radiation, surgery, and targeted therapy compared

Noticeable country-wise variation in treatment cost

Advanced therapies show higher average cost globally

5️⃣ Tumor Size Distribution

Majority of diagnosed cases fall under 2–4 cm tumor size band

Larger tumor size correlates with advanced stage diagnosis
